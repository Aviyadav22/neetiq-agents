# Structural Patterns — NeetiQ Voice Audit

36 structural red flags. Each one, if found, is a flag even if no individual word is on the banned list.

## Punctuation patterns
1. Em dash (`—`) or en dash (`–`) anywhere. Zero tolerance.
2. Double hyphen (` -- `) used as dash. Zero tolerance.
3. Semicolons in social posts. Zero tolerance.
4. Three or more ellipses (`...`) in a post.
5. Multiple exclamation marks (`!!`, `!!!`).

## Opener patterns
6. Opens with "In today's" / "In the world of" / "In the realm of" / "In an era of".
7. Opens with "I'm thrilled" / "Excited to announce" / "Proud to share" / "Thrilled to share".
8. Opens with a single-word sentence followed by a period (e.g., "Wow." "Huge.") — OK rarely, flag if used more than once per post.
9. Opens with a rhetorical question that answers itself in the next line.
10. Opens with a stat and no human context (e.g., "83% of companies…" with no "I" anchor). In Phase 1–2 this breaks Avi's voice.

## Three-part parallel structures (classic AI)
11. Three short phrases on separate lines for dramatic effect:
    ```
    No rules.
    No limits.
    No kidding.
    ```
    Flag any 3-line sequence of ≤5-word lines.
12. "Not X. Not Y. Not Z." structure.
13. "It's not just X. It's Y. It's Z." structure.
14. "X, meet Y." headline structure.

## "Here's the" patterns
15. "Here's the thing:" / "Here's what's wild:" / "Here's the kicker:" / "Here's why:" / "Here's the deal:".
16. "Here's what I learned" / "Here's what happened" in openers.
17. "This is why…" as the wrap-up in the last sentence.

## CTA patterns (LinkedIn-template tells)
18. "What do you think?" as the final sentence.
19. "Agree or disagree?"
20. "Let me know in the comments."
21. "Tag someone who needs to see this."
22. "Would love your thoughts."
23. "Drop a 🚀 if you agree" or any emoji-CTA.

## Listicle / format tells
24. Bullet list inside a social post (lines starting `-`, `*`, `•`, or numeric `1.` / `2.`).
25. Numbered list with dramatic build-up ("Here are 5 lessons I learned…").
26. All-caps words as emphasis (beyond acronyms like DPDP, SC, HC, BCI).
27. Fake-dialogue structure ("Boss: X. Me: Y.").
28. Checkmark emoji lists (✅ ✅ ✅).

## "It's not just X, it's Y" and cousins
29. "It's not just X, it's Y" (explicit).
30. "More than just X, this is Y".
31. "This isn't about X. It's about Y."

## Vague authority tells
32. Claims without a number or source ("tons of companies are asking about this…").
33. "As someone who has been in legal tech for…" (Avi is 2 years in, don't overclaim).
34. "After years of…" (Avi doesn't have years yet).
35. "Studies show…" without citing one.

## Over-structured closings
36. A final sentence that paraphrases the opening sentence for symmetry (AI loves this).

## Examples of correct, Avi-voiced post opens (PASS)

- "Two High Courts in India just barred judges from using AI to write orders. Fair."
- "81% of Indian companies haven't updated their privacy policies for DPDP."
- "Spent 3 hours last Tuesday reading a 47-page vendor agreement. A clause about data sharing is buried on page 32."
- "Anthropic just launched AI contract review inside Word. First line in the docs: 'will hallucinate citations.'"

## Examples of failed openers (FAIL)

- "In today's rapidly evolving legal landscape…" (patterns 6, 1 if followed by em dash)
- "I'm thrilled to share that…" (pattern 7)
- "Here's the thing: legal AI is broken." (pattern 15)
- "Legal tech. It's not what it used to be." (pattern 8 + a cliché)
